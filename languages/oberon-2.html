Oberon-2 is an extension of the original Oberon programming language that adds limited reflection and object-oriented programming facilities, open arrays as pointer base types, read-only field export and reintroduces the FOR loop from Modula-2. It was developed in 1991 at ETH Zurich by Niklaus Wirth and Hanspeter MÃ¶ssenbÃ¶ck, who is now at Institut fÃ¼r Systemsoftware (SSW) of the University of Linz, Austria.
